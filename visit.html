Ladybower Reservoir

Ladybower Reservoir is a large, man-made reservoir located in the Upper Derwent Valley of Derbyshire, England. It is one of three reservoirs in the area, the other two being Howden Reservoir and Derwent Reservoir, collectively known as the Derwent Valley Reservoirs. Ladybower was completed in 1945 and is a key component of the Derwent Valley Aqueduct system, supplying water to the cities of Derby, Leicester, Nottingham, and Sheffield.

Ladybower was constructed between 1935 and 1943, and its construction involved the flooding of two villages, Ashopton and Derwent, which were subsequently submerged beneath the reservoir's waters. The project was a significant engineering undertaking and played a crucial role during World War II, when the reservoir was used by the Royal Air Force (RAF) for practicing low-level flying. The skills developed here were later employed in missions such as the famous Dam Busters raid in 1943.

Ladybower Reservoir has become a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The surrounding area offers picturesque views and opportunities for outdoor activities, including walking, cycling, and birdwatching.
